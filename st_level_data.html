STATE-LEVEL VISUALIZATIONS

2012-2016 Manufacturing Differences by State

This interactive map displays absolute differences for each of our four variables from 2012-2016. While the default view is for the entire manufacturing sector, we can also filter to 3 and 4-digit NAICS Codes if desired. Texas had a staggering decrease in receipts of 179 billion over that span, and Texas, California, and New York all lost a substantial amount of employees as well. From a payroll perspective, we can see that there are increases in most states, which aligns with our conclusions discussed at the national level.

2012-2016 Manufacturing Percent Changes by State

This map provides a similar view, but instead visualizes the percent changes for each state. While New Mexico's difference in receipts was not particularly notable, the percent change is significant, decreasing by 56% from 2012 to 2016. Meanwhile, Nevada and Oregon had impressive gains. With regards to number of employees, Louisiana, Wyoming, and again New Mexico had large percentage decreases over the five years. Like the above map, we can also drill down to lower NAICS levels for even more data if needed.

2016 Variable Relationships by State

These two tree maps demonstrate the relationships between receipts and costs, and payroll and employees for the latest year of manufacturing data (2016). Texas, California, Ohio, and Michigan appear to be dominant forces in the manufacturing sector, while Alaska, Wyoming, Hawaii, and DC don't have much of a presence. As we expect, in general the two variable pairings have positive relationships - that is, as one increases we expect the other to increase. One example of a notable difference to this trend is Animal Food Manufacturing, where five states have very similar employee counts despite differences in payroll.
